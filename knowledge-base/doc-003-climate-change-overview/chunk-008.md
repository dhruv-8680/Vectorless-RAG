Adaptation Strategies

Even with aggressive mitigation, some degree of climate change is already locked in. Adaptation strategies help societies cope with unavoidable impacts.

Infrastructure resilience: Building sea walls, improving drainage systems, designing buildings for extreme heat, and upgrading power grids to handle increased demand and extreme weather.

Agricultural adaptation: Developing drought-resistant crop varieties, adjusting planting schedules, improving irrigation efficiency, and diversifying food sources.

Early warning systems: Improving weather forecasting and disaster preparedness to reduce casualties from extreme events.

Ecosystem-based adaptation: Preserving and restoring natural ecosystems (wetlands, mangroves, forests) that provide natural protection against floods, storms, and erosion while also sequestering carbon.

Water management: Improving water storage, recycling, and desalination capacity to address changing precipitation patterns and increased drought risk.
