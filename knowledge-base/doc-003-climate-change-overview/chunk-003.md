Global Temperature Rise

The global average temperature has increased by approximately 1.1°C above pre-industrial levels as of 2023. While this may seem small, even modest temperature changes have cascading effects on weather patterns, ecosystems, and human systems.

The 10 warmest years on record have all occurred since 2010. Climate models project that without significant emissions reductions, global temperatures could rise by 2.5°C to 4.5°C by 2100. Even the most optimistic scenarios with aggressive mitigation suggest warming of at least 1.5°C.

Temperature rise is not uniform — Arctic regions are warming 2-3 times faster than the global average, a phenomenon known as Arctic amplification. This is caused by ice-albedo feedback: as ice melts, darker ocean water is exposed, which absorbs more heat, causing more melting.
